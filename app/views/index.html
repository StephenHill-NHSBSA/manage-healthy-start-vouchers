Manage Healthy Start Scheme Version 1.0

The Healthy Start scheme allows eligible applicants to get help to buy milk, food and vitamins.

The 'Manage' service allows operational staff to manage the scheme e.g. Answering queries, changing details, adding new applicants etc...

Private Beta (Sprint 1)

Contact Centre applications (16 - 17 year olds)

Hypothesis A

We believe that giving 16 - 17 year olds the ability of applying for the scheme via the contact centre will be a useful feature for all those applicants who are unable to apply online e.g. because of lack access to the internet or the necessary digital skills as it will ensure everyone who needs the service can use it in a way that meets their needs. This will ensure that nobody who needs to access the service is 'left behind'.

What we created:

  • Added the ability for a CCS agent to add an applicant who is 16 - 17 and Pregnant (view user flow).
  • Amended the prototype to correctly demonstrate the NHS BSA's chosen login method (Azure AD).
  • Based off initial CCS feedback, we amended the 'Add Applicant' journey so that the questions aren't asked from a third-party perspective e.g. 'What's their date of birth?', instead they are asked as if CCS were reading them to the applicant e.g. What's your date of birth?. This reduces the cognitive load on a CCS agent and is more of a natural conversation.
  • Again based off initial CCS feedback, we also added in a unique reference number on the confirmation page. Third party research suggests that not only does it help CCS staff find an application, but it also reassures the applicant that their application is being dealt with and helps to reduce the number calls to the contact centre.

What we plan to test:

  • We believe that it's better for an applicant to be told they aren't eligible as quickly as possible - therefore this workflow confirms eligibility and only when that is confirmed, do we check their identity. Does that contradict the current workflow that a CCS agent currently does i.e. do they check identify first then check their eligibility?
  • Does the 'one thing per page' pattern for adding a new applicant slow down a CCS agent's ability of checking / adding somebody onto the scheme?
  • Does the grouping of information relating to an applicant e.g. Contact details, Relationship details etc... make sense to CSS agents? Would they group things differently? Card sorting exercise to determine this.
  • How does the process work when a CCS agent needs to transfer the call to another colleague - or even better - a completely different provider e.g. DWP? How is details of the call shared between them?
