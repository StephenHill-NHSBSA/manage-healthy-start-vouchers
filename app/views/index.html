Private Beta (Sprint 1)

Contact Centre applications (Under 18 & Pregnant ONLY) Increment 1
Create a new application for a user in the contact centre using a 'throw-away' UI

Hypothesis A

We believe that giving Under 18 year olds the ability of applying for the scheme via the contact centre will be a useful feature for all those applicants who are unable to apply online e.g. because of lack access to the internet or the necessary digital skills as it will ensure everyone who needs the service can use it in a way that meets their needs. This will ensure that nobody who needs to access the service is 'left behind'.

What we created:

  • After confirming their eligibility via IVR, workshops with CCS staff have shown that their natural workflow is to search for a claim first (using First name, Last name and Postcode) and then if a record doesn't exist, they then add one.
  • Because of this, search functionality was added and if a record isn't found, the agent has the opportunity of using the search details to create a new record.
  • CCS staff will need to capture the following to make a successful claim; name, due date, address and date of birth. Other optional fields will include; National Insurance number, mobile phone number and email address.
  • If they provide us with an email address, we send them an email to confirm details of the benefit. If not, and they provide a mobile phone number instead we send them a confirmation by text. Finally, we also confirm the same details when the card is delivered.

What we plan to test:

  • Does the UI hinder CCS being able to deal with a phone applicant effectively?
  • If an ineligible applicant gets through the IVR filtering questions, does validation stop them before they are able to make a successful claim?
  • Can eligible applicants recall their due dates whilst on the phone?
  • Are the verbal terms and conditions succinct enough to not delay an outcome? Can full terms and conditions be sent with the card?

Private Beta (Sprint 0)

Contact Centre applications (Under 18 & Pregnant ONLY) Increment 1
Pre-check eligibility

Hypothesis A

We believe that pre-checking an applicant's eligibility via IVR (Interactive Voice Response) before they reach a member of staff at the Contact Centre will be a useful feature for all applicants as well as Contact Centre staff as it will ensure that only applicants who meet our Private Beta criteria are passed onto the BSA, and everyone else is diverted to Serco (the current telephone route). This will reduce the amount of call time which does not result in a successful claim.

What we created:

  • Drafted an IVR User Flow which will allow applicants who meet our Private Beta criteria (Under 18 & Pregnant for the first time) to apply via the Contact Centre.
  • Users who do not meet this criteria will either be transferred to NHS BSA (current card users), or Serco (current / prospective voucher users).
  • Using this flow, an actual IVR Prototype was created using Amazon Connect.

What we plan to test:

  • Does the IVR Prototype successfully... allow eligible applicants to apply?
  • Does the IVR Prototype successfully... stop ineligible applicants from applying?
  • Does the IVR Prototype successfully... transfer current users who receive Healthy Start vouchers to Serco to manage?
  • Does the IVR Prototype successfully... transfer current users who receive Healthy Start cards to NHS BSA to manage?

What we found:

  • It's widely understood that the benefits system is confusing for people so it should be no surprise that people were confused with the term 'vouchers'. Consider changing to 'benefit'? i.e. Do you already receive Healthy Start benefit?
  • We found the cognitive load on users was quite high e.g. Are you 10 weeks pregnant or more?, so perhaps consider making the questions more generic e.g. Are you pregnant?, and try to mitigate this with promoting the eligibility criteria prior to ringing the call centre, as well as stopping ineligible people from applying via the CMS.

Any known issues?

  • For the purposes of the prototype we will not actually transfer eligible / ineligible users to NHS BSA or Serco, instead, they will hear the message: "You will now be transferred to NHS BSA / Serco" and the call will disconnect.

Manage Manage Healthy Start Scheme (v4)
Version 3

Hypothesis A

We believe that giving 16 - 17 year olds the ability of applying for the scheme via the contact centre will be a useful feature for all those applicants who are unable to apply online e.g. because of lack access to the internet or the necessary digital skills as it will ensure everyone who needs the service can use it in a way that meets their needs. This will ensure that nobody who needs to access the service is 'left behind'.

What we created:

  • Added the ability for a CCS agent to add an applicant who is 16 - 17 and Pregnant (view user flow).
  • Amended the prototype to correctly demonstrate the NHS BSA's chosen login method (Azure AD).
  • Based off initial CCS feedback, we amended the 'Add Applicant' journey so that the questions aren't asked from a third-party perspective e.g. 'What's their date of birth?', instead they are asked as if CCS were reading them to the applicant e.g. What's your date of birth?. This reduces the cognitive load on a CCS agent and is more of a natural conversation.
  • Again based off initial CCS feedback, we also added in a unique reference number on the confirmation page. Third party research suggests that not only does it help CCS staff find an application, but it also reassures the applicant that their application is being dealt with and helps to reduce the number calls to the contact centre.

What we plan to test:

  • We believe that it's better for an applicant to be told they aren't eligible as quickly as possible - therefore this workflow confirms eligibility and only when that is confirmed, do we check their identity. Does that contradict the current workflow that a CCS agent currently does i.e. do they check identify first then check their eligibility?
  • Does the 'one thing per page' pattern for adding a new applicant slow down a CCS agent's ability of checking / adding somebody onto the scheme?
  • Does the grouping of information relating to an applicant e.g. Contact details, Relationship details etc... make sense to CSS agents? Would they group things differently? Card sorting exercise to determine this.
  • How does the process work when a CCS agent needs to transfer the call to another colleague - or even better - a completely different provider e.g. DWP? How is details of the call shared between them?
